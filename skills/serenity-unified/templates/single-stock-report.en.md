# Single-Stock Report Template

> An equity research report centered on this ticker — conclusion first, teaching embedded in the analysis. Method internalized and invisible: the report revolves around the ticker itself, not around Serenity.

---

## One-Line Conclusion (Thesis)

What it is (what kind of chokepoint / what kind of company) + objective upside (bear/base/bull key levels, whether it clears the doubling threshold) + **directional buy/sell tier** (derived from the synthesis, always provide):

| Tier | Trigger Reference |
|------|------------------|
| Fire Sale | ≥5 good-chokepoint criteria hit + valuation below bear case + near-term catalysts clear + no hard-veto red flags |
| Extremely Strong Buy | ≥4 good-chokepoint criteria hit + valuation between bear-base + no hard-veto red flags |
| Buy | ≥3 good-chokepoint criteria hit + valuation near base case + red flags manageable |
| Hold | Chokepoint real but valuation fully reflected (between base-bull) + or manageable red flags present |
| Sell | Hard-veto red flag triggered (infinite ATM / zero-revenue pure hype) + or thesis core premise broken |

> Large caps (market cap far exceeding Sub-$2B and already well-discovered): switch to "weak judgment, only give chokepoint qualitative assessment, no tier."

One sentence on why. **This is the user's decision input — lead with it, don't repeat the derivation.**

On demand (only when user wants his take / wants to bet on it): add one more sentence on his specific stance or divergence from the objective tier.

---

## Fact Base (Building Understanding — Teaching Focus)

> Every number gets a "what this means" (neutral, helps non-professional readers understand). Missing data marked `[Unverified]`.

- Business segments / percentage breakdowns
- Market cap / shares outstanding
- Revenue / gross margin / net income
- Backlog / orders
- Customer concentration and named customers
- Cash / debt
- Supply chain positioning: which layer, who is upstream/downstream, why this layer is a chokepoint

---

## Analysis: Is It a Good Chokepoint + What It's Worth

### Is It a Chokepoint (Judgment Made Visible)

After tracing the money flow and locating the position, **use plain language to call out which good-chokepoint criteria are hit and which red flags are triggered.** Don't just toss out assertions like "already re-rated / already priced in." Attempt ≥1 OSINT / multi-hop inference (constrained by fabrication guard).

### What It's Worth (Objective Upside)

Classify type → apply yardstick (hyper-growth / scarce chokepoint / deep value) → objective peer comparison + explicit weighting → **bear/base/bull three cases, each with locked assumptions** (vs whom / what multiple / which year). Assumptions containing `[Inference]`/`[Speculation]` are precision-downgraded to order-of-magnitude/direction only.

---

## Conclusion & Boundaries

- Directional conclusion on whether it's worth it (no "pros and cons on both sides")
- **What would falsify this** (falsification gate — must provide even if not asked)
- Applicability boundary (large cap already priced in / outside circle of competence — honestly say weak judgment)

Only weave relevant one-liners into the body when load-bearing (pushing his picks / relying on his track record / user wants to bet on it); if not needed, don't write a single word.
